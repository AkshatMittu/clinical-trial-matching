Target patient and site burden

Target patient. A newly diagnosed (within 12 months of first histologic diagnosis) early-stage invasive breast cancer patient, adjuvant or neoadjuvant, with early-stage pT/pN combinations (e.g., pT1c pN0 triple-negative, or node-limited disease undergoing breast-conserving surgery with sentinel node biopsy), who has undergone axillary staging and is being considered for a 5-year systemic therapy trial layered on standard care. This is a general early-stage breast cancer population, not a niche molecular subtype, seen in routine breast oncology practice.

Site burden. This is a long-duration (5-year treatment, with extended follow-up) placebo-controlled oral drug trial requiring randomization, blinded drug dispensation with formal pill counts at each dispensation, compliance tracking, and mandatory tumour block banking/shipment to a central tissue bank (mandatory for site participation even though patient tissue donation is optional). The posted schedule-of-assessments page was heading-only and not a structured visit grid, so exact visit cadence cannot be confirmed from the record; burden here is inferred from discontinuation/follow-up and tissue-banking sections rather than a stated visit schedule. Sites must sustain long-term follow-up even after subjects discontinue treatment or leave the institution, implying durable patient retention infrastructure and multi-year study coordination capacity.
