Target patient and site burden

Target patient. Women or men with HR-positive, HER2-negative, locally advanced or metastatic breast cancer whose tumor harbors a PIK3CA mutation, who progressed on/shortly after adjuvant endocrine therapy (AI or tamoxifen) and have received NO prior systemic therapy for metastatic disease — i.e., a first-line metastatic HR+/HER2- population enriched for a specific genomic biomarker, requiring central and/or local PIK3CA testing before enrollment.

Site burden. Based on the posted Schedule of Assessments heading (weak evidence, heading-only confidence), the protocol requires: baseline collection of both blood and tumor tissue for central PIK3CA testing (with liquid biopsy fallback via FoundationOne Liquid CDx if local results unavailable), tumor imaging every 8 weeks for 2 years then every 12 weeks, PK blood sampling at multiple timepoints, close AE monitoring including a distinct hyperglycemia follow-up pathway (inavolisib is a PI3K inhibitor with known hyperglycemia risk), multiple patient-reported outcome instruments (QLQ-C30, QLQ-BR23, BPI-SF, PRO-CTCAE, EQ-5D-5L) administered per schedule, long-term survival follow-up approximately every 3 months post-discontinuation, and optional mobile-nursing infrastructure at approved sites. This implies a site needs biomarker testing/specimen logistics, an established infusion/oral-oncolytic administration capability, PRO data capture systems, and experience managing metabolic AEs (hyperglycemia) associated with PI3K inhibitors — but the exact visit-by-visit grid was not available in the provided material.
