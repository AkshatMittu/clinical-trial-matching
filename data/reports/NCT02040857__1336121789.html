Target patient and site burden

Target patient. Adult men and pre/postmenopausal women with histologically confirmed HR+/HER2-negative Stage II (except T2N0) or Stage III invasive breast cancer, in the adjuvant setting, who have already demonstrated tolerance to at least one month of tamoxifen or an aromatase inhibitor and have a plan for at least 2 years of continued endocrine therapy. Patients may have received prior (neo)adjuvant chemotherapy or radiotherapy but must be past acute toxicity. This is a curative-intent, early-stage adjuvant population, not metastatic disease.

Site burden. Based on the Schedule of Assessments (weak evidence — heading matched but page not a structured grid), the protocol involves monthly visits for the first 4 cycles, then every 8 weeks through Cycle 12, then every 12 weeks through Year 2 (24 cycles total, ~2 years), with serial CBC with differential (including Day 15 draws in early cycles), ECGs at multiple timepoints for QTc monitoring, drug diaries and pill counts at every visit, and paper-based QOL surveys (EORTC QLQ-C30) at defined intervals. Sites need oncology infusion/clinic infrastructure capable of frequent labs and ECGs, on-site or networked lab capability with fax-back result review before dosing, research staff for drug diary/pill count adherence tracking, and biospecimen handling/shipping (including a defined dry-ice/cryovial workaround for Friday/holiday draws) coordinated through a central CaTissue tracking system.

Site burden was read from the posted protocol's Schedule of Assessments (page 54, confidence: heading_only).
