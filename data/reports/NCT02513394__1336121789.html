Target patient and site burden

Target patient. Adult men and women (pre- or postmenopausal) with newly diagnosed Stage II (with a cap on Stage IIA) or Stage III HR+ (ER+/PR+) / HER2-negative early invasive breast cancer, who are within 12 months of diagnosis, either about to start or already up to 6 months into adjuvant endocrine therapy, and who have completed any neo/adjuvant chemo or biologic therapy with resolved toxicity. This is a mainstream adjuvant early breast cancer population seen broadly in breast oncology practice, not a rare or heavily pretreated subgroup — the differentiator for site selection is volume and infrastructure, not niche disease expertise.

Site burden. This is a long-duration adjuvant trial: 2 years of active oral IP (palbociclib, 28-day cycles) with on-site Cycle Day 1 dispensing/compliance visits at a defined cadence (C1, C2, then every 3 cycles), followed by a multi-year follow-up phase (Q6-month then annual contacts) extending out to 10 years with periodic blood/plasma/cfDNA collection at protocol-specified long-term timepoints (e.g., years 5, 7, 10). Requires mandatory submission of an FFPE tumor tissue block to a central repository before randomization, serial hematology/chemistry monitoring (relevant to palbociclib-associated neutropenia), PRO and adherence questionnaires at multiple cycles, and long-term retention of patients across a decade of follow-up. Site must support drug accountability/IP dispensing infrastructure and long-term specimen/data logistics as part of a large multinational cooperative group study (AFT/ABCSG/BIG/PrECOG/NSABP).

Site burden was read from the posted protocol's Schedule of Assessments (page 58, confidence: grid).
