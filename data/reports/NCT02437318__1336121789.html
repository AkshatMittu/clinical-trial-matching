Target patient and site burden

Target patient. A postmenopausal woman (or man) with HR-positive, HER2-negative advanced/metastatic breast cancer whose disease has progressed on or after aromatase inhibitor therapy, with no more than one prior line of endocrine therapy for metastatic disease and no prior chemotherapy for advanced disease, no prior fulvestrant, and no prior PI3K/mTOR/AKT inhibitor exposure. A meaningful subset of these patients must carry a PIK3CA mutation, as the primary endpoint is PFS in the PIK3CA-mutant cohort, meaning the site must be able to identify and enroll molecularly-selected HR+/HER2- advanced breast cancer patients.

Site burden. Per the posted Schedule of Assessments, the site must support an extended screening window (up to 35 days) with tumor tissue/mutation workup, frequent early on-treatment visits (Cycle 1 Day 1, 8, 15; Cycle 2 Day 1, 15), then monthly visits through subsequent cycles, with fasting labs (chemistry, glucose, HbA1c, lipids, lipase/amylase), coagulation panels, serial ECGs, periodic cardiac imaging (MUGA/ECHO) through 36 months, and imaging-based tumor assessments every 8 weeks for 18 months then every 12 weeks — a substantial, multi-year infusion (fulvestrant IM) plus oral drug administration and safety-monitoring burden requiring both infusion/injection capacity and close metabolic/cardiac monitoring infrastructure (relevant given alpelisib's hyperglycemia risk).

Site burden was read from the posted protocol's Schedule of Assessments (page 96, confidence: grid).
